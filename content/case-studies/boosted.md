## Headline

From concept to reality: the impact of user research on the product vision

## Subtitle

Helping geoscientists find relevant seismic data for their exploration project from multiple historical databases

## Context & Objectives

- Helping geoscientists quickly access relevant seismic data for oil exploration projects.
- Boosted Geoscientist is a ranking tool that analyzes, cleans, and classifies seismic data to identify reference data for making better assessments and predictions.
- Previously, users spent more time searching for relevant data rather than analyzing and interpreting it, as the data was scattered across various databases with varying quality.

## Challenges

The idea of creating a database where geoscientists can easily access relevant seismic data for their oil exploration projects is not new. Various initiatives over the past 30 years aimed at cleaning and organizing this data have failed for various reasons.

The challenge lies in creating a tool that adds value right from the start of its development, thereby encouraging its adoption and ultimately achieving the goal of building this reference database.

## Business Value

- Invest in the right oil exploration opportunities
- Improve the success rate of exploration
- Enhance the performance of all geoscientists (saving man-days)
- Save on data storage costs

## Target & Method

- 27 Geoscientists & 8 Data Managers
- 8 User Interviews
- 2 Personas
- 1 Value Proposition Canvas
- Co-design Workshops
- 10 User Tests
- SUS (System Usability Scale)
- Basic UX Analytics with App Insight

## Product Team

- 2 UX/UI Designers (including myself)
- 1 Product Owner (PO), 1 Project Manager (PM), and 1 Scrum Master
- 1 Tech Lead, 2 Full Stack Developers, 2 Data Engineers, and 1 Data Scientist

## Impact

Initially, the focus was heavily on building a reference database to help users easily find the reference data. However, after conducting user interviews, we found that there were several ways to address their needs.

We then adjusted our product strategy into two main modules:

- The first module focused on developing a powerful search and filtering tool that could connect to multiple data sources. This solution provides immediate value to users, even without a reference database. It allows them to more easily find relevant data before starting a new exploration project.
- The second module involved building the reference database using the first module. After using the tool to target a set of potentially relevant data for their project, users were asked to tag this reference data. However, this didn't work as well as expected, as users had to analyze and interpret the data in another tool, and they didn't return to Boosted Geoscientist to tag the data.

Here are several interesting KPIs to assess the impact of the two modules:

- ~30 unique users per month (target = 42 users)
- More than 15 databases integrated into the tool
- 7.38 TB removed from 16.12 TB scanned
- 2,637 seismic data points marked as reference
- Approximately 40% of created projects have no reference data
- Time savings are not measurable but are reported by users
- SUS = 84/100 (~7) from a total of 18 responses

## Personal Development

- First experience in product strategy: I loved participating in breaking down and prioritizing the product vision into several value blocks.
- We were truly able to co-create the tool with the users, who were extremely enthusiastic and available. This experience allowed me to understand the fundamental distinction between a product that first addresses a business need, which also interests users, and a product that truly meets users' needs and, in doing so, generates monetary value.
- I would have liked to stay on the case during its development to further explore the interactions between the two tools and find a solution with less friction regarding data tagging.
- I also realized the difficulty of getting processes or tools adopted that offer medium-term value but have a lesser short-term impact.
