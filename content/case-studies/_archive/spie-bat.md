## Headline

Harmonizing construction industry processes through the design of a digital activity report

## Subtitle

Rethinking Spie Batignolles' activity report using design thinking to redefine each module and harmonize business processes

## Context & Objectives

- Spie Batignolles took advantage of the implementation of a new ERP system to reorganize and harmonize its processes across various modules, including Personnel and Equipment Tracking, Procurement, and Project Monitoring.
- Our mission was to understand and challenge the current processes for each module of the activity report, then design and iterate on a new digital solution.
- The goal of the activity report was to simplify their previous tools, improve performance, and ease data entry, which was often manual and very time-consuming.

## Challenges

The real challenge of this project was precisely to help the client redefine these processes through design thinking. The client wanted to avoid a top-down approach to process harmonization, favoring an approach based on the needs of the field and the users.

## Target & Method

Our team gathered the needs of the operational staff for each module of the project through interviews, stakeholder mapping, and "as-is" user journeys.

During workshops, we used projective scenarios and mockups to co-create the solution with our users, ensuring that the activity report reflected the reality of the field and their needs.

- 12 User interviews
- 16 workshops
- 4 Stakeholder mappings
- 4 User journeys
- 12 User tests

## Product Team

- We did not work with an Agile framework on this project.
- 1 Lead UX Designer & 2 Junior UX/UI Designers (including myself)
- About ten people responsible for the ERP implementation.

## Impact

- Streamlined procurement workflows, reducing request processing time through centralised processes
- Reduced manual data entry by implementing auto-fill functionality
- Redesigned user centric workflows gathered from on-site construction crews

## Personal Development

- First long-term assignment with a client.
- I gained immense learning through mentoring from the Lead UX Designer.
- Notable progress in UI/Visual design thanks to my role as an executor and the large number of screens I worked on.
- I am very pleased with my progress as a facilitator: at the beginning, I was afraid of making mistakes and hesitant to speak up during workshops with clients. By the end of the assignment, I took leadership on certain less critical topics.
